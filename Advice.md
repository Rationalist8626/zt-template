Cold and Flu

Return for review if:

Your condition gets worse or does not get better in two weeks

Have cough with thick phlegm that contains some blood

Have difficulty in breathing

------------------------------

Diarrhoea and Vomiting

How to treat at home

Drink more water or clear fluids. Avoid coffee, alcohol, spicy, fried or fatty foods, and dairy products.

Do not take solid food until vomiting has stopped.

Return for review if:

Your condition gets worse or does not get better in a few days

Have vomiting that affects your ability to eat and drink

Have high fever of 38Â°C and above that persists for more than 3 days

------------------------------

Fever

Use a thermometer to take your temperature

Drink plenty of water to stay hydrate

Return for review if:

Your fever lasts more than 3 days

------------------------------

Back Pain

Complete rest in bed is not recommended.

Exercise will not worsen degeneration but will help strengthen muscles to protect joints.

Degenerative changes on imaging are not always the cause of pain.

Modify your activities. Pain should improve in 6 weeks.

------------------------------

Cuts and Open Wounds

How to treat at home

Wash your wounds with clear water or normal saline as soon as possible to remove dirt and debris.

Apply an appropriate dressing and firm pressure to the wound area to stop the bleeding if necessary.

Apply non-adherent dressing to the wound.

Keep the wounds clean and review them daily, looking out for signs of infection.

Return for review if:

The wound shows no signs of improvement after more than a week has passed

You notice signs of an infection.

------------------------------

Headaches

Return for review if:

You get headaches frequently (i.e., more than 15 days in a month)

You are experiencing more nausea or vomiting

------------------------------

High blood pressure

Avoid excessive salt intake in your diet

Exercise regularly and get plenty of rest

Take BP 1-week prior to TCU. Optimal home BP is below 135/85.

Return for review if:

Blood pressure readings consistently higher than your usual

------------------------------

Insect Bites and Stings

Return for review if:

You notice signs of an infection, such as developing a fever, persistent redness and pain, swelling, or pus forming in the wound

------------------------------

Mild Burns and Scalds

Return for review if:

Nosebleeds

How to treat at home

Sit down and lean forward

Using your thumb and index finger, pinch the nose just above the nostrils

Breathe through your mouth

Continue until the bleeding stops, usually after 10 minutes

If the bleeding continues, hold for another 10 minutes and suck on some ice cubes.

Go to the emergency department if you are experiencing:

Your nosebleed does not stop after 20 minutes

Your nosebleed is caused by injury to the nose, such as being hit in the face by a fist or object

------------------------------

Sleep Hygiene

Avoid smoking, caffeine, large meal or water intake.

Keep a consistent sleep schedule. Do not nap. Regular exercise.

------------------------------

Skin Infections

Go to the emergency department if you are experiencing:

High fever and chills

Severe pain

Painful Blisters

Rapid progression of the swelling

Painful swelling of joints affecting movement

------------------------------

Sore Eyes

Avoid touching or rubbing the affected eye

Wash your hands with soap and water frequently

Do not share towels or pillows with others

Do not wear any eye makeup or contact lenses until your symptoms clear up

Hold a warm, damp cloth over your eyelids to relieve discomfort

Return for review if:

If your condition gets worse or does not get better in 3 days

There is pain or pus discharge from the eye

Go to the emergency department if you are experiencing:

Your vision is affected

------------------------------

Strains and Sprains

How to treat at home

Stop doing the activity that caused the strain or sprain immediately

Administer the PRICE therapy:

Protect the injured area

Rest

Ice the area by placing an ice pack (wrap a bag of ice in a towel) on the injured area for 15 to 20 minutes

Compress the injured area with an elastic bandage until the swelling stops

Elevate the injured area by raising it above the level of your heart, especially at night

Return for review if:

You cannot put any weight on the injured limb

Symptoms have not improved after 3 days of treatment at home
